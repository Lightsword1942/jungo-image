Step 2. Create the Parental Control Rules and Schedules


Limit Access by: What’s This

“Limit Access by” means you need to choose how to limit access to your computer(s)/device(s) that you added to the “Selected Devices” list above. You may limit access by choosing one of the following:

1. Block a list of Websites or Embedded Keywords

2. Allow a specific list of Websites or Embedded Keywords

3. Block ALL Internet access.


Blocked or allowed websites are identified either by their specific website address (Example: www.example.com) OR by embedded keywords inside their website address (Example: “sample” within www.sampleproduct.com).


For example, if you wanted to block www.example.com and all websites with the word “sample” in the website address, you would first click on “Block a list of Websites or Embedded Keywords.” Next, you would type in www.example.com in the “Website” list and sample in the “Embedded Keyword within a Website” list.


You may add as many Website and Embedded Keyword items as you want simply by typing or pasting each into its Website or Embedded Keyword field, one at a time. Click the Add button to add each item to your Blocked Items or Allowed Items list. If you need help with choosing a list of Websites or Embedded Keywords within Website addresses, we suggest searching existing child-friendly sites that contain such lists.

To Remove a Website or Embedded Keyword from your Blocked Items or Allowed Items list, click on the item you wish to remove then click the Remove button. You may remove more than one at a time.


IMPORTANT: The router does not block or allow based on content of websites, emails, or attachments, only based on website addresses or keywords embedded in those website addresses.


Note 1: You may find it easier to create a rule to allow a limited list of known websites than to create rules to block certain websites.


Note 2: You can only create one rule for each computer(s)/ device(s).
